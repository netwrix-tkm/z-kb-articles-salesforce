Comments:
Mount points and regular directory junctions are indistinguishable, and we generally do not want to follow directory junctions as they lead to files that would otherwise be scanned, while volume mount points contain files not otherwise accessible.
As a heuristic, it seems reasonable not to follow junctions that have the System and Hidden attributes set, such as the backward-compatibility junctions added in Windows Vista and beyond such as "My Documents" and "Application Data".

FSAA will follow mount/junction points. FSAA will not follow if the system and hidden attributes are set on the mount/junction point.

Instructions:

  1. Close all instances of StealthAUDIT.
  2. Update all DC files in your %SAInstallDir%DC folder
  3. Update all PrivateAssemblies in your %SAInstallDir%PrivateAssemblies folder.
  4. Launch StealthAUDIT. Run the 'Update Proxy Service' query under the Maintenance tab in the FSAA data collector against your proxy servers (if running proxy service).
